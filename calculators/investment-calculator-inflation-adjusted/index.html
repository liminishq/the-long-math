Investment Calculator – Inflation Adjusted

This calculator estimates investment growth with recurring contributions and compounding. It shows inflation-adjusted (real) results as the primary output, reflecting purchasing power in today's dollars, while also displaying nominal results for comparison.

All dollar amounts you enter—starting balance and contributions—are assumed to be in today's dollars. The calculator applies an assumed long-term inflation rate to show how purchasing power evolves over time, making it easier to understand the real value of your future balance.

The calculator defaults to showing real (inflation-adjusted) results as the primary output, with nominal results shown as a secondary comparison. This approach helps you see both the dollar amount and the actual buying power of your investment over time.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does "inflation-adjusted" mean?

Definition:

Inflation-adjusted values show what money is worth after accounting for changes in prices over time.

In this calculator, inflation-adjusted results are expressed in today's dollars, so amounts can be compared based on purchasing power rather than raw dollar totals.

Example:

A loaf of bread that costs $3 today and $6 in the future has not become twice as valuable. The price has increased (doubled) over time.

The same adjustment applies to investments. If a portfolio grows from $100,000 today to $5 million over the same period, its inflation-adjusted value would be closer to $2.5 million in today's dollars because the cost of living has - in this example - doubled.

The inflation rate used here is a long-term assumption. You can explore historical inflation rates by year to see how inflation has varied over time and to help contextualize the assumption used in this calculator.
