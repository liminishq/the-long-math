Tools and Calculators

This page lists the interactive financial calculators currently available on The Long Math. Each calculator models a specific long-term financial question using explicit assumptions and inspectable arithmetic. Tools are updated independently and may differ in scope, inputs, and jurisdiction.

Some calculators are accompanied by explanatory articles that describe the arithmetic in more detail.

Arithmetic-first calculators with explicit assumptions and inspectable outputs.

New calculators are in development and will be uploaded when available.

Last updated: February 3, 2026

  • Canada Personal Income Tax Calculator 2025

    Estimates Canadian personal income tax with transparent breakdowns. Shows federal and provincial tax calculations, CPP/EI contributions, dividend gross-ups, capital gains inclusion, and detailed "show the math" sections.

  • Cost of a Financial Advisor (AUM Model - most common)

    Quantifies the long-term impact of advisory fees and lost compounding using explicit assumptions and inspectable math.

  • Flat-Fee or Hourly Advisor Cost

    Calculates the true cost of flat-fee or hourly advisor fees, including fees paid and lost compounding, with AUM-fee equivalent comparison.

  • Portfolio Growth Simulator

    Historical outcome simulator for multiple investment vehicles using public data sources. Compare cash, bonds, equities, and more over time with monthly contributions.

  • Simple Investment Calculator

    Models compound growth with recurring contributions. Calculate final balance, total invested, interest earned, and IRR. Optional inflation-adjusted (real) results. No fees. Inspectable arithmetic.

  • Mortgage Calculator — Payments, Interest, and Amortization

    Calculate mortgage payments, remaining balance, and total interest over the full amortization period. See how payments and costs evolve over time with interactive graphs.

  • How Much Extra Return Is Required to Offset Investment and Management Fees

    The additional annual return required to fully offset investmenting and management fees over time.

  • Active vs Passive Break-Even

    Compares overall returns of active vs passive investments and real returns required to break even.

  • Fees Hub

    Four fee questions with short answers and links to the canonical calculators.

Scope and limitations

Calculators on this page are designed for educational inspection of arithmetic relationships. Jurisdiction, tax year, assumptions, and data sources vary by tool and are stated on each calculator page.

Disclaimer: The calculators and tools at The Long Math are provided for educational purposes only and are not intended to constitute financial or tax advice. Results are based on simplified models and user-supplied assumptions. Users are recommended to complete their own due diligence.
