How this loan repayment calculator works

This calculator models a fixed-rate, fully amortizing loan.

Each payment consists of:

  • Interest, calculated on the remaining balance
  • Principal, which reduces the balance

Early payments are mostly interest.

Later payments are mostly principal.

The total interest cost depends on rate, term, and payment frequency.

The math used (no hidden assumptions)

Let:

  • P = loan amount (principal)
  • r = annual interest rate (as a decimal)
  • m = payments per year (12 monthly, 26 biweekly, 52 weekly)
  • i = r / m = interest rate per payment
  • N = years × m = total number of payments

The payment per period is calculated as:

Payment = P * [ i(1+i)^N ] / [ (1+i)^N - 1 ]

If the interest rate is zero, payments are simply:

Payment = P / N

For each payment:

  • Interest = remaining balance × i
  • Principal = payment − interest
  • New balance = old balance − principal

The amortization table, charts, and totals are all derived from this same calculation.

Assumptions and limitations

  • Fixed interest rate for the entire loan term
  • Interest compounds at the payment frequency selected
  • No fees, penalties, insurance, or taxes included
  • Payments are rounded to cents for display
  • Final balance is clamped to zero to avoid rounding noise
